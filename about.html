Sara R. Dean

Sara grew up in Connecticut and graduated from Eastern Connecticut State University in Fall 2016 with a B.A. in history and social sciences. She currently lives in Boston, where she is pursuing an M.A. in history with a concentration in public history at Northeastern University.

Sara's recent scholarship has been in the field of 19th-century American cultural history. Recently, Sara published an article about Polish immigration to New Britain, Connecticut, at the turn of the twentieth century (“‘Planting Christian Virtues’: Reverend Lucyan Bójnowski and Media Coverage of New Britain’s Polish Immigrant Community, 1897-1916.” Germina Veris. 2016). Her scholarly interests extend beyond history as well. She is currently working on an article about J. R. R. Tolkien's career in philology and its influence on his worldbuilding process in the Lord of the Rings trilogy.
