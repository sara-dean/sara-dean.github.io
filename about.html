Sara R. Dean

Sara grew up in Connecticut and graduated from Eastern Connecticut State University in Fall 2016 with a B.A. in history and social sciences. She currently lives in Boston, where she is pursuing an M.A. in history with a concentration in public history.

Sara's recent scholarship has been in the realm of 19th-century American cultural history. In Spring 2017, Sara published an article called "Planting Christian Virtues": Reverend Lucyan Bójnowski and Media Coverage of New Britain’s Polish Immigrant Community, 1897-1916" in Eastern Connecticut State University's history journal, Germina Veris. Her scholarly interests extend beyond the field of history as well--she is currently working on an article about J. R. R. Tolkien's career in philology and its influence on his worldbuilding process in the Lord of the Rings process.
