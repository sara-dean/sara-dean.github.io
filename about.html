Sara R. Dean

Sara grew up in Connecticut and graduated from Eastern Connecticut State University in Fall 2016 with a B.A. in history and social sciences. She currently lives in Boston, where she is pursuing an M.A. in history with a concentration in public history. Sara's recent scholarship has been in the realm of 19th-century American cultural history. In Spring 2017, Sara published an article called "Planting Christian Virtues": Reverend Lucyan Bójnowski and Media Coverage of New Britain’s Polish Immigrant Community, 1897-1916" to Eastern Connecticut State University's history journal, Germina Veris. She is currently working on an article about J. R. R. Tolkien's career in philology and the way it influenced his worldbuilding in the popular Lord of the Rings trilogy.
